## Steps
1. Preheat the oven to 180°C.
2. Blend the peeled tomatoes with their juice in a saucepan. Add the garlic grated on a microplane, the olive oil, the sugar and the bay leaves. Warm over minimum heat, just long enough to dissolve the sugar and infuse the bay — the sauce should not reduce.
3. Cut the eggplants into slices about 4 mm thick.
4. Spread them across 3 trays, spray both sides with olive oil and bake at 180°C until tender and browned, swapping the trays' positions three times for even cooking.
5. Assemble in a medium baking dish, discarding the bay leaves: a little sauce, a layer of eggplant, grated parmesan (reserve some for the top), then repeat. Finish with sauce.
6. Bake for 30 minutes at 180°C (convection).
7. Switch the oven to the grill at full power. Cover the top with the reserved parmesan and grill for about 2 minutes, watching closely.
8. Scatter with shredded fresh basil before serving.
<Tip>It's even better the next day: cook it ahead without grilling, cover and refrigerate, then reheat and grill the top just before serving.</Tip>
